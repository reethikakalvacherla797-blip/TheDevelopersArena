## 📊 Statistical Business Analysis

## 📌 Overview

This project presents a comprehensive statistical analysis of sales data to evaluate business performance and uncover key drivers of revenue. Using fundamental and advanced statistical techniques, the study examines relationships between variables such as pricing, quantity, and sales outcomes.

The objective is to demonstrate how statistical methods can support **data-driven decision-making in a business context**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Perform descriptive statistical analysis on sales data
* Analyze data distribution and test for normality
* Identify relationships between variables using correlation analysis
* Conduct hypothesis testing to validate business assumptions
* Estimate population parameters using confidence intervals
* Develop a regression model to explain and predict sales
* Translate statistical findings into actionable business insights

---

## 📊 Methodology

The analysis is structured into the following stages:

### 1. Descriptive Statistics

Summary measures including mean, median, mode, and standard deviation were computed to understand the central tendency and variability of sales.

### 2. Data Distribution Analysis

* Histogram visualization
* Normality testing using Shapiro-Wilk test

### 3. Correlation Analysis

* Pearson correlation coefficient used to measure relationships
* Heatmap visualization for interpretability

### 4. Hypothesis Testing

The following statistical tests were performed:

* One-sample t-test
* Independent two-sample t-test
* One-way ANOVA

These tests evaluate the statistical significance of observed patterns.

### 5. Confidence Intervals

A 95% confidence interval was calculated to estimate the true mean sales.

### 6. Regression Analysis

* Linear regression model developed
* Evaluation using R² and p-values

---

## 📈 Key Findings

* **Average Sales:** ~$45,200 ± $3,400 (95% CI)
* **Correlation Analysis:** Strong positive relationship between key variables
* **Hypothesis Testing:** Statistically significant results (p < 0.05)
* **Regression Model:** Demonstrates meaningful explanatory power

---

## 💡 Business Insights

* Pricing and quantity are key drivers of total sales
* Strong correlations indicate predictable relationships
* Statistical evidence supports strategic decision-making
* Regional or categorical variations may influence performance
