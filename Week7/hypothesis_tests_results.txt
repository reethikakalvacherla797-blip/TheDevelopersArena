
HYPOTHESIS TEST RESULTS


Dataset: sales_data.csv
Confidence Level: 95% (α = 0.05)


1. ONE-SAMPLE T-TEST

Objective:
To determine whether the average sales differ significantly from the benchmark value (200,000).

Null Hypothesis (H0):
Mean Sales = 200,000

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
Mean Sales ≠ 200,000

Result:
p-value = 1.529271713575926e-11

Conclusion:
Since p-value < 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis.

Interpretation:
There is strong statistical evidence that the average sales are significantly different from 200,000.

2. TWO-SAMPLE T-TEST (REGION COMPARISON)

Objective:
To compare average sales between two regions.

Null Hypothesis (H0):
Mean Sales (Region A) = Mean Sales (Region B)

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
Mean Sales (Region A) ≠ Mean Sales (Region B)

Result:
p-value = 0.7226811638407535

Conclusion:
Since p-value > 0.05, we fail to reject the null hypothesis.

Interpretation:
There is no statistically significant difference in average sales between the two regions.

3. ONE-WAY ANOVA (MULTIPLE REGIONS)

Objective:
To determine whether there are significant differences in sales across multiple regions.

Null Hypothesis (H0):
All group means are equal

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
At least one group mean is different

Result:
p-value = 0.09723678084979993

Conclusion:
Since p-value > 0.05, we fail to reject the null hypothesis.

Interpretation:
There is no strong statistical evidence of significant differences in sales across regions.

OVERALL INTERPRETATION

- The average sales are significantly different from the benchmark value.
- Regional differences in sales are not statistically significant.
- Sales performance appears consistent across regions.
- Statistical testing confirms that observed variations are likely due to random variation rather than systematic differences.
